# Deal with Collars

## 1. Analyze the Situation

Before taking any action, determine whether the removal attempt is reasonable and within hands of the players.

- Is the person attempting to remove the collar capable of doing so?
- Is it a self-removal attempt?
- Are they using extreme measures (cutting off a hand, leg, wrist) to escape the collar?
- What is the context for the scene?
- What information do we have on the remote holder and/or the prisoner?

Remember to ask for context and informations before making a decision.

---

## 2. Collar Tampering

If a player is attempting to interfere with a collar (through artificing or similar):

- Inform all involved parties that the remote holder will be alerted.
- This includes:
  - The remote holder.
  - The prisoner.
  - The individual attempting to free the prisoner.
  - Others present in the scene.
- If the warden (remote holder) is online, they must be ticketed and alerted of the situation (the remote will alert them).
- They are free to decide if they wish to contest or not; a scene-lock may be requested until they are present.
- Once present in the scene, they are free to continue and escalate from there.

---

## 3. Self-Escape Attempts

If the prisoner is attempting to escape on their own:

- Analyze how long they have been a prisoner, what are the future plans of the faction for them and who is their remote holder.
- If they are approved for a escape
    - Narrate the escape appropriately.
    - If other characters are present, they may attempt to pursue or stop the escape.
    - If no one is present to intervene, the prisoner is considered to have escaped and the collar may be removed.
- If they are not approved, remind them of the Prisoner Rules (insert link for rules, forum or otherwise).

:::warning

Escape attempts must still comply with all prisoner guidelines.

Self-escape attempts often require consideration of several factors, including:

- Magi strength
- Knowledge of collars and their operation
- Time spent imprisoned
- Any other relevant contextual factors

Always request and review scene logs before making a final decision.

:::

---

## 4. Removing the Collar

Once the situation has been properly reviewed and approved:

1. Debug the prisoner.
2. Open their `bag` variable.
3. Open the collar variable.
4. Set the `energy` variable to 10.

The collar will automatically fall off on the next tick.
